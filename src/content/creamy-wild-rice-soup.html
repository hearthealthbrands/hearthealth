Most "creamy" soups are a heart health nightmare. They rely on heavy cream or full-fat coconut milk, both of which are loaded with saturated fat that can drive up LDL cholesterol.

But we shouldn't have to choose between "heart-healthy" and "comfort food."

The Solution: The "Cannellini Hack"

Instead of dairy, we take a can of organic white kidney beans (cannellini) and blend them with a bit of broth until smooth. When you stir this into the soup, it creates a rich, opaque, velvety broth that mimics heavy cream perfectly.

But unlike cream, this mixture is packed with soluble fiber‚Äîthe specific type of fiber that binds to cholesterol in your gut and removes it from your body. You aren't just eating soup; you are actively scrubbing your arteries.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Lundberg Wild Rice
  • 6 cups Bone Broth
  • 1 can Cannellini Beans
  • 8 oz Baby Bella Mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp Graza Olive Oil
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 small Yellow Onion, diced
  • 2 ribs Celery, diced
  • 2 medium Carrots, diced
  • 1 large Sweet Potato, cubed
  • 1.5 tbsp Cajun Nation
  • 2 cups Fresh Kale
  • Pinch of Sea Salt

Instructions

1

The Saut√©

Heat the Graza Olive Oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery and saut√© for 5-7 minutes until soft. Add the garlic, mushrooms, and Cajun Nation seasoning. Cook for another 3 minutes until fragrant.

2

The Simmer

Add the sweet potato, Wild Rice, and Bone Broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes, or until the rice is tender.

3

The Cream Hack

While the soup simmers, take your can of Eden Cannellini Beans. Pour half the beans into a blender with a ladle of the hot broth (or water). Blend on high until completely smooth and white. Keep the other half of the beans whole.

4

The Finish

When the rice is cooked, stir in the Bean Cream mixture and the whole beans. This will instantly thicken the soup. Toss in the Kale and let it wilt for 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
