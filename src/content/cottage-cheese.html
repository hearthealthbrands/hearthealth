Nutrition Protocol

The HeartHealth Case for Cottage Cheese

It’s currently the internet's favorite high-protein snack. But to make it heart-healthy, you have to choose the right tub.

"A portable food that offers lots of health benefits... if you choose correctly."

Cottage cheese is having a massive resurgence in the fitness community due to its incredible protein-to-calorie ratio.

For the HeartHealth community, this can be a Tier 1 Superfood, but only if you follow one specific rule. If you buy the wrong tub, it’s a sodium bomb. If you buy the right one, it’s a lipid-lowering secret weapon.

Why We Eat It: The "Slow" Protein

According to the Cleveland Clinic, cottage cheese is rich in Casein protein. Unlike whey (which digests fast), casein digests slowly, releasing amino acids over several hours.

  • Curbs Appetite: It prevents the "grazing" urge that leads to snacking on processed carbs.
  • Blood Sugar Control: The high protein/low carb ratio helps prevent insulin spikes.

The Selection Strategy

Standard "4% Milkfat" cottage cheese can be high in saturated fat. To unlock the heart benefits without the lipid penalty, Loyola Medicine explicitly recommends choosing low-fat dairy.

The Protocol Rule:

Always buy Low Fat (2% or 1%) or Fat Free. This eliminates the saturated fat risk while keeping 100% of the protein benefit.
