Cottage cheese is having a massive resurgence in the fitness community, and for good reason. It is incredibly high in protein and low in calories.

However, for the HeartHealth community, it falls into a specific category: Tier 2 Snack. It is an excellent tool for satiety, but it comes with two major risks: Sodium and Saturated Fat.

Why We Eat It: The "Slow" Protein

According to the Cleveland Clinic, cottage cheese is rich in Casein protein. Unlike whey (which digests fast), casein digests slowly, releasing amino acids over several hours.

  • Curbs Appetite: It prevents the "grazing" urge that leads to snacking on processed carbs.
  • Blood Sugar Control: The high protein/low carb ratio helps prevent insulin spikes.

The Heart Risk

Loyola Medicine explicitly warns that full-fat dairy can increase LDL-C (Bad Cholesterol).

Standard "4% Milkfat" cottage cheese can have 3g-5g of saturated fat per serving. Additionally, many brands are sodium bombs (400mg+), which is dangerous for blood pressure management.

The Protocol Rule:

You must buy Low Fat (2% or 1%) or Fat Free, and always check the sodium label.
