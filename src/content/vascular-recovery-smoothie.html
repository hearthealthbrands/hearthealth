This is not your average "gym bro" shake. It is a precision-engineered Hybrid Protocol designed to address the two critical needs of the athletic heart patient: muscle repair and arterial protection.

The "Hybrid" Strategy

We utilize Grass-Fed Whey Isolate for its superior amino acid profile and rapid absorption‚Äîperfect for the post-workout anabolic window.

However, we mix it with Organic Soy Milk instead of water or cow's milk. Why? Because soy protein is the only protein source with an FDA health claim for lowering cholesterol. By combining the two, we get the muscle-building power of whey with the lipid-lowering power of soy isoflavones.

Combined with the nitric oxide from the spinach and the anthocyanins from the berries, this smoothie actively helps lower blood pressure and prevents LDL oxidation.

The Protocol

Yields: 1 Serving Prep: 5 Mins Format: Blender

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 cup Silk Organic Soy Milk (Unsweetened)
  • 2-3 cups Fresh Spinach (Packed tight)
  • 1 cup Frozen Mixed Berries

The Macros

  • 1 scoop Grass-Fed Whey Isolate (French Vanilla)
  • 1 tbsp Almond Butter (Unsweetened)
  • 1 tbsp Organic Ground Flaxseed

Instructions

1

Liquid First

Pour the Silk Soy Milk into the blender first. This lubricates the blades and ensures the protein powder doesn't stick to the bottom.

2

Liquify The Greens

Add the Spinach next. Pulse or blend on low for 10 seconds just to break down the leaves. This prevents "chunky" spinach texture in the final drink.

3

Add Solids & Blend

Add the Transparent Labs protein, almond butter, Spectrum flaxseed, and frozen berries. Blend on high for 45-60 seconds until completely smooth. The purple berries will overtake the green color.

üìù Optimization Tips

  • Temperature: If you prefer it colder/thicker, add 2-3 ice cubes, but be aware this may dilute the flavor slightly.
  • Flax Storage: Ensure your ground flaxseed is stored in the fridge or freezer. The oils in flax go rancid quickly at room temperature!
  • Sweetness: The French Vanilla protein plus the berries usually provides plenty of sweetness without needing extra dates or honey.
