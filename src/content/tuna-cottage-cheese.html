High Protein Lunch

The Mayo-Free Tuna Salad

Traditional tuna salad is a nutritional paradox: healthy fish swimming in inflammatory soybean oil (mayonnaise). We fix the ratio by swapping in cottage cheese for higher protein and better texture.

"The easiest way to ruin a high-Omega-3 meal is to cover it in cheap mayo. This protocol fixes that."

Cottage cheese is the secret weapon here. It mimics the creamy texture of mayonnaise but provides a slow-digesting Casein protein base that keeps you full for hours.

By adding avocado (monounsaturated fat) and a rainbow of vegetables (fiber), we turn a simple pantry staple into a complete metabolic meal.

The Protocol

1

The Drain (Crucial)

Open the 2 cans of tuna. Drain as much water as physically possible. If the tuna is too wet, the salad becomes soupy. Press the lid down into the can to squeeze out moisture.

2

Prep the Veg

Finely dice the red bell pepper and avocado. Quarter the cherry tomatoes. The goal is to have all pieces roughly the same size for the perfect bite.

3

Mix the Base

In a large bowl, combine the drained tuna and 1 cup of cottage cheese. Mash them together slightly with a fork to incorporate the textures.

Why? Mixing the protein first ensures the cottage cheese coats the tuna evenly before the delicate veggies are added.

4

Fold & Season

Add the corn, bell pepper, tomatoes, avocado, and olives. Stir in the Dijon mustard and Cajun seasoning.

Taste before salting! Cottage cheese and canned tuna naturally contain sodium, so you likely won't need extra salt. Add black pepper to taste.

Serving Suggestion

Serve inside large Romaine lettuce leaves for crunch, or scoop with cucumber slices to keep it grain-free.

Ingredients

2 cans (5oz) Wild Albacore Tuna (in water)
1 Cup Low-Fat Cottage Cheese
1/2 Large Avocado (diced)
2/3 Cup Red Bell Pepper (diced)
1 Cup Cherry Tomatoes (quartered)
2/3 Cup Sweet Corn (thawed)
1 can (4oz) Sliced Black Olives
2 tsp Dijon Mustard
1 tsp Cajun/Creole Seasoning
