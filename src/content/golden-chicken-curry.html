The Coconut Ban

Traditional curry relies on coconut milk, which is over 80% saturated fat (the kind that stiffens arteries). We swap this for a tempered Greek Yogurt emulsion. You get the same silky texture with zero saturated fat and a massive protein boost.

The "Bioavailability" Hack

The Recipe

Yields: 4 Servings Prep: 10 Mins Cook: 25 Mins

Ingredients

The Protein & Veg

  • 1 lb Chicken Breast, cubed
  • 1 can (15oz) Chickpeas, rinsed
  • 3 cups Fresh Spinach
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 Yellow Onion, diced

The Golden Sauce

  • 3/4 cup 0% Greek Yogurt (Room Temp)
  • 1 cup Low Sodium Chicken Broth
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp Fresh Ginger, grated
  • 2 tsp Ground Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper (Crucial!)

Instructions

1

The Sear

Heat olive oil in a deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the cubed chicken and onion. Saut√© for 5-6 minutes until the chicken is golden and the onions are translucent.

2

The "Bloom"

Add the garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, and black pepper. Stir constantly for 60 seconds.
Lipid Lab Note: Heating the spices in the oil ("blooming") releases the essential oils and makes the antioxidants more potent.

3

The Simmer

Pour in the chicken broth and add the rinsed chickpeas. Scrape the bottom of the pan to release any flavorful browned bits. Simmer for 5 minutes until the liquid reduces slightly.

4

The Temper (Don't Skip!)

Turn the heat off completely. Place your Greek yogurt in a separate bowl. Take a spoonful of the hot curry sauce from the pan and mix it into the yogurt. Repeat this 3 times.
Why? This warms the yogurt slowly so it doesn't curdle when added to the pan.

5

Finish & Fold

Pour the tempered yogurt mixture back into the skillet. Add the fresh spinach and stir gently until the sauce is creamy yellow and the spinach has wilted (about 1 minute). Serve immediately.
