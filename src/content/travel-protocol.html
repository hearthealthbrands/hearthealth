Lifestyle Protocol

The Travel Protocol

Airports are designed to spike your sodium and inflammation. The only way to win is to pack your own defense.

"The hardest place to maintain a heart-healthy diet is Terminal B."

Travel is the number one reason people fall off their lipid-management protocols. Airport food is notoriously high in sodium (bad for blood pressure, especially at altitude) and seed oils (inflammatory).

Relying on finding a "healthy salad" at the gate is a gamble. The solution is to treat your carry-on like a mobile pantry.

The "Kit" Strategy

I pack a specific kit for every flight. It meets three criteria:

  • Shelf Stable: Must survive 8+ hours in a bag without refrigeration.
  • TSA Approved: No liquids or gels over 3.4oz.
  • Lipid Friendly: High protein and Omega-3s, low saturated fat and sodium.
1

The Protein Anchor: Salmon Jerky

Beef jerky is often loaded with sodium and saturated fat. Fable Fish makes a Wild Alaskan Salmon jerky that gives you 31g of protein plus a massive dose of marine Omega-3s (EPA/DHA) which are anti-inflammatory.

2

The Healthy Fat: Organic Walnuts

The only tree nut with significant ALA Omega-3s. I buy the raw, unsalted bags from NOW Foods. They are filling, quiet to eat, and great for the brain.

3

The Bridge: RXBAR

When you just need calories. No B.S. ingredients (Egg whites, dates, nuts). I stick to the Blueberry or Sea Salt Chocolate flavors.

4

The Hot Meal: Oatmeal Cups

Travel Hack: Bring a dry Bob's Red Mill oatmeal cup. Ask the flight attendant for hot water. Instant, high-fiber meal that binds cholesterol.
