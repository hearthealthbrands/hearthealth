Heart Health Wins

The new guidelines get several things right that will actively lower your triglycerides and inflammation.

  • 1. Zero Added Sugar: Eliminating processed sugar stops the production of small, dense LDL particles (the dangerous kind).
  • 2. Protein Focus: Higher protein supports lean mass, which improves insulin sensitivity‚Äîa key factor in overall heart risks.

The LDL Trap

For those genetically prone to high cholesterol (hyper-responders), the new fat recommendations are risky.

  • 1. Saturated Fat Load: Butter, tallow, and fatty beef downregulate LDL receptors in the liver, causing cholesterol to linger in the blood.
  • 2. Fiber Gap: By crushing grain intake, many people accidentally eliminate their only source of soluble fiber, which scrubs LDL.

The Bottom Line

The new inverted pyramid is a reaction to the processed food epidemic, and in that regard, it is correct. But biology is nuanced.

If you are tracking LDL-C or ApoB, you cannot blindly follow the "Butter is Back" mantra. Use the structure of the pyramid to eliminate sugar and processed junk, but be smarter about your fats.
