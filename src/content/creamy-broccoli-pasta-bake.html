Creamy pasta bakes are usually a cholesterol nightmare‚Äîloaded with heavy cream and fatty cheeses. We fixed that by using a "stealth" ingredient that adds creaminess and protein without the artery-clogging fat.

The "Stealth" Technique

The secret weapon here is Low-Fat Cottage Cheese. When blended with broth and herbs, it transforms into a silky, rich sauce that mimics heavy cream perfectly.

By using a 1% or 2% milk fat variety, we drastically cut the saturated fat while skyrocketing the protein content.

We also doubled down on fiber by using Brown Rice Pasta and doubling the volume of spinach. Fiber is essential for binding to cholesterol in the digestive system and removing it from the body.

The Functional Ingredients

The Recipe

Yields: 4 Servings Prep: 10 Mins Cook: 40 Mins

Ingredients

The Sauce (Blender)

  • 3 cups Low-sodium chicken or veg broth
  • 16 oz Low-fat cottage cheese (2% or 1%)
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 2 tsp Garlic powder
  • 2 tsp Dried oregano
  • 1 tsp Dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp Kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp Black pepper

The Bake

  • 12 oz Dry brown rice penne
  • 4 cups Baby spinach, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups Frozen broccoli florets
  • 1/4 cup Grated parmesan
  • 3/4 cup Shredded part-skim mozzarella

Instructions

1

Prep & Blend

Preheat the oven to 400¬∞F. Lightly spray a 13 x 9-inch casserole dish with oil. Add the broth, cottage cheese, lemon juice, garlic powder, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper to a blender. Blend until completely smooth (about 30 seconds).

2

Combine

Add the dry penne, frozen broccoli, chopped spinach, parmesan cheese, and the blended cottage cheese mixture directly to the casserole dish. Mix well until the penne is submerged in the liquid.

3

Bake

Cover tightly with foil (or a lid) and bake until the pasta is just al dente, about 30 to 35 minutes. *Note: Gluten-free pasta brands vary, so check doneness at 30 mins.*

4

The Cheese Finish

Turn the oven to high broil. Remove the foil and sprinkle the shredded part-skim mozzarella over the top. Broil until the cheese is golden and bubbling (4 to 5 minutes). Watch closely so it doesn't burn!

5

Rest & Serve

Remove from the oven and let sit, covered, for at least 15 minutes. This is crucial‚Äîit allows the pasta to absorb any excess liquid and creates the perfect creamy texture.

üìù Kitchen Notes

  • Storage: Refrigerate in a sealed container for up to 4 days. Freeze for up to 3 months.
  • Reheating: From refrigerator, microwave for 2 minutes or heat in a saucepan over medium heat. If frozen, thaw in the fridge overnight first.
  • Protein Boost: Add shredded rotisserie chicken or cooked ground chicken to the bake for an even higher protein meal.
