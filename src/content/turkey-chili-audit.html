The LDL Audit: Why It Works

üßΩ

The "Sponge Effect"

We use two cans of kidney beans for a reason. Soluble fiber acts like a sticky sponge in your gut, binding to bile acids (made of cholesterol) and removing them from the body. This forces your liver to pull LDL out of your blood to replenish them.

‚öñÔ∏è

The 99% Non-Negotiable

Most "turkey chili" recipes use 85/15 meat, which has more saturated fat than some beef. We strictly use 99% Extra Lean turkey. This keeps the flavor savory but eliminates the inflammatory fat that downregulates your LDL receptors.

üåä

Sodium Control

High sodium stiffens arteries, making them more susceptible to plaque. By choosing "No Salt Added" tomatoes and thoroughly rinsing the beans, we cut the sodium load significantly without losing the rich chili taste.

The Recipe

Yields: 6 Servings Prep: 10 Mins Cook: 45 Mins

Ingredients

The Fresh & Lean

  • 2 tsp Olive Oil
  • 1 lb Extra Lean Ground Turkey (99% Fat Free)
  • 1 Yellow Onion, chopped
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced

The Pantry & Spices

  • 2 cans (15oz) Low Sodium Kidney Beans (Rinsed!)
  • 1 can (15oz) Sweet Corn
  • 1 can (28oz) Crushed Tomatoes (No Salt Added)
  • 1.25 cups Low Sodium Chicken Broth
  • 4 tbsp Mild Chili Powder
  • 2 tsp Cumin & 1 tsp Oregano

Instructions

1

Saut√© Veggies

Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add onion, garlic, and red pepper. Saut√© for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently until softened.

2

The "Lean" Brown

Add the ground turkey and break it up.
Cooking Tip: Since 99% lean turkey has very little fat, do not overcook it here! As soon as the pink is gone, move immediately to the next step.

3

Spice & Simmer

Add chili powder, cumin, oregano, cayenne (optional), and salt. Stir for 20 seconds to bloom the spices.

4

The Fiber Load

Pour in the tomatoes, chicken broth, rinsed kidney beans, and corn. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-45 minutes until the chili thickens and the flavors meld.
