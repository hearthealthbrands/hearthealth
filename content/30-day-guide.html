Your First 30 Days: A Simple Guide

This isn't a list of rules. It's a clear, week-by-week plan to help you take control with confidence.

Week 1

Focus on Addition

Your Goal: Add Soluble Fiber

Start by adding powerful, LDL-lowering foods into your diet. Soluble fiber acts like a sponge, binding to cholesterol and removing it from your body.

Start your day with a bowl of oatmeal or barley.
Swap one snack for an apple, pear, or a handful of edamame.
Add a side of Brussels sprouts or asparagus to one dinner.

Week 2

Master the Fats

Your Goal: Incorporate Healthy Fats

Fat isn't the enemy—the type of fat is what matters. This week is about swapping "bad" fats for heart-healthy ones from avocados, nuts, and olive oil.

Switch from cooking with butter to using olive oil.
Add a quarter of an avocado to a salad or piece of toast.
Snack on a small handful of almonds or walnuts.

Week 3

Power Up with Plants

Your Goal: Introduce Plant Sterols & Stanols

You don't need to become a vegetarian. Simply incorporating more powerful plant-based foods is one of the most effective strategies for lowering LDL.

Look for foods fortified with plant sterols (like certain margarines or orange juices).
Try one "Meatless Monday" by swapping ground beef for lentils or tofu.
Snack on a cup of steamed, salted edamame (soy protein).

Week 4

Build Your System

Your Goal: Identify Your "Go-To" Meals

You've built powerful new habits. Now it’s time to create a sustainable routine that eliminates daily stress and decision-making.

Identify one breakfast you love and can make quickly (e.g., oatmeal with berries).
Find two easy, healthy lunch options (e.g., a big salad with chicken; leftovers).
Master three simple, delicious dinners (e.g., baked salmon with asparagus).
